As an employer, one of your priorities will be making sure that your team is as productive as possible in helping you achieve your company’s goals. But sometimes an employee may develop a theme of poor timekeeping and absence.

While there may be many justifiable reasons for poor punctuality and excessive sick days, it’s down to you and your employee to resolve any underlying issues to ensure that the business doesn’t take a hit over the lack of productivity that tardiness leads to.

The importance of punctuality is even more imperative in customer-facing businesses, where workers need to be present to serve shoppers in specific timeframes or else the customer experience will suffer.

While the 21st Century has seen something of a shift towards more flexible working hours in many offices, there are still countless environments where it’s impossible to be so lax with timing – and in these cases, the offending employee would need to be approached about their behavior accordingly.

Remember that your employee is human, and as such will have innumerable concerns, distractions and motivations at play under the skin. Perhaps their absenteeism is down to relationship troubles or a long-term family illness? Plenty of companies encourage their workers to leave their problems at the door when they enter the workplace, but sometimes a member of staff will feel overawed and unable to maintain their focus. 228/400 words

How will you help develop the young person’s teamwork and communication skills?

Communication is the key to success – humans are social animals designed to thrive in collaborative circumstances. However, some of us are more adept at communicating than others and it’s the failure to share thoughts, ideas and feelings effectively which can lead to uncomfortable situations.

Creating and maintaining teams within a working situation can be key to the success of a business. Bringing a diverse group of people together to work towards a common goal, for example increasing sales outcomes, ensures that different ideas and approaches to reaching the desired outcome can be evaluated and implemented. At least that’s the idea!

The reality can be that often one or two individuals can dominate a group and effectively override everyone else, so it’s beholden on those managing teams to ensure this is not allowed to happen.

Is it a good idea to have people of varying temperaments, abilities and views in one team? Of course it is, because without disagreements there’d be no discussions and without discussions nothing could ever change.

Taking extremes of homogenised teams: a team comprised solely of risk takers could potentially gamble the future success of the business by always insisting the riskiest approaches are best.

Conversely a conservatively focused team would never stray away from the straight and narrow, the tried and tested. 216/400 words
