Employability support provided for Kickstart Scheme workers

As an employer, one of your priorities will be making sure that your team is as productive as possible in helping you achieve your company’s goals. But sometimes an employee may develop a theme of poor timekeeping and absence.

While there may be many justifiable reasons for poor punctuality and excessive sick days, it’s down to you and your employee to resolve any underlying issues to ensure that the business doesn’t take a hit over the lack of productivity that tardiness leads to.

The importance of punctuality is even more imperative in customer-facing businesses, where workers need to be present to serve shoppers in specific timeframes or else the customer experience will suffer.

While the 21st Century has seen something of a shift towards more flexible working hours in many offices, there are still countless environments where it’s impossible to be so lax with timing – and in these cases, the offending employee would need to be approached about their behavior accordingly.

Remember that your employee is human, and as such will have innumerable concerns, distractions and motivations at play under the skin. Perhaps their absenteeism is down to relationship troubles or a long-term family illness? Plenty of companies encourage their workers to leave their problems at the door when they enter the workplace, but sometimes a member of staff will feel overawed and unable to maintain their focus. 228/400 words
