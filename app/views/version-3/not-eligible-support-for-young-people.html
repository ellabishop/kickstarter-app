Your Kickstart Scheme job placements need to include employability support

You need to provide employability support to young people to get a Kickstart Scheme grant.

The type of support we expect is anything that will help a young person be in a better position to find work in the future.

The Kickstart Scheme job placements should help them get basic work skills. This includes:

  • attendance
  • timekeeping
  • communication
  • teamwork
  • travelling to work

Kickstart participants may also need help with:

  • CV and interviews
  • looking for long term work

You can also work with other organisations to provide this employability support on your behalf.

Apply for the Kickstart Scheme grant when you have included employability support with your job placements.

Read more about the Kickstart Scheme on the guidance page.
